The Choice Foundation

The Choice Foundation is a compassionate organization dedicated to helping individuals overcome substance abuse and reclaim their lives. Founded in memory of Cody, who struggled with addiction, the foundation provides support, rehabilitation options, and financial assistance to those in need. It emphasizes the importance of personal choice in the recovery journey, encouraging individuals to take control and seek a better future. Through its efforts, the Choose Foundation aims to create lasting change and offer hope to those battling addiction...

About Us

The Choice Foundation, Inc. is a 501(c)(3) organization, established in 2008 for the purpose of enriching the lives of those living in the Greater Houston area.

Our programming focuses on health and human services to support families, initiatives that assist youth in their educational and career development, projects that expand artistic abilities and cultural awareness through visual and performing arts, and international initiatives that promote awareness of the world and its people.

Over the past decade, The Choice Foundation, Inc. has raised over a half-million dollars to support educational and community programs throughout the Houston area, including working with community partners to support and encourage student interest in STEM, partnering with groups that address food inequities and promote health and wellness in low income communities, and programs that excite students interests in the arts.

We are pleased to have partners like the Missouri City (TX) Chapter of The Links, Incorporated, Houston Methodist Hospital, HillDay Public Relations, Inc. and others whose mission is aligned with ours on programs that help to strengthen our Houston area communities.

Community Impact

The Choice Foundation is committed to supporting education, social services, and civic and cultural initiatives in Houston area communities that enrich the lives of those in need.

Through our partnership with the Missouri City (TX) Chapter of The Links, Incorporated, professional women of color volunteer their time and expertise to encourage student interests in STEM, educate and provide resources to the elderly, collaborate with educators on the importance of arts education to inspire creativity in students, highlight the important work of our Houston area international refugees, promote health, and engage and empower individuals and communities to choose healthy behaviors that reduce the risk of developing chronic diseases.

our Partnership

Our partnership with Houston Methodist Hospital and the Missouri City (TX) Links enabled us to touch students at Attucks Middle School and the Cuney Homes housing development through the Kids Xplore program to foster their enthusiasm in STEM and provide hands-on opportunities for participants to apply and practice age appropriate computational and problem solving skills.

Our partnership is a foundation built on trust, collaboration, and shared vision. Together, we unite strengths, creating meaningful opportunities and fostering innovation. Through mutual respect and dedication, we drive progress, ensuring lasting success. By supporting each other, we overcome challenges and celebrate victories. With a commitment to growth and excellence, our partnership thrives, shaping a future where creativity and ambition lead the way.

Our Choice Awards

Held biennially since 1993, the Our Choice Awards is The Choice Foundation’s Signature fundraiser that recognize individuals for their exemplary contributions and achievements to community service. Their work reflect a sincere commitment to provide services and activities that enhance the quality of life for youth, seniors, and the underserved in our community and abroad. Honorees have provided long-term and transformational service to the local community in five areas.

ARTS AWARD

Honorees provide experiences in the visual and performing arts that enrich and inform students and adults about the importance of history, and foster creativity.

SERVICES TO YOUTH AWARD

Honorees provide opportunities for secondary students to explore STEM-related careers, to apply for college scholarships, and to visit area colleges.

NATIONAL TRENDS AND SERVICES AWARD

Honorees provide information to targeted senior populations on topics such as voter registration, senior resources, and health maintenance activities, as well as access to fresh produce.

Health and Human Services Award

Honorees provide information and learning opportunities on health related issues which significantly impact our intergenerational community, including diabetes, heart disease, breast cancer, and healthy lifestyles.

International Trends and Services Award

Honorees provide services or supplies to international communities including Haiti, as it continues to address devastation and recovery from natural disasters, and locally, to our Houston international refugees.

UMBRELLA AWARD

Honorees have demonstrated exemplary community service and leadership and all other facets of service, collectively.
